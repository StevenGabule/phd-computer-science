# Portal Navigation Guide — Top 10 Target Programs

**Purpose:** Per-program application portal walkthrough so you know what to expect before opening the form. Saves the 20-30 minutes per program of figuring out the quirks.
**Use timing:** Phase 4 (Oct-Dec 2027) Task 65.

**⚠️ Verify everything before applying.** Portal URLs change between admissions cycles. Verify each URL at the program's official admissions page (linked below) before creating an account.

---

## Common patterns across most programs

Before reading per-program quirks, know the patterns most programs share:

- **Account creation:** Usually requires institutional email + password. Confirmation email arrives within 1 hour.
- **Document upload:** PDF only (most), max 2-5 MB per file. Some accept Word; verify.
- **Required documents (almost universal):** Statement of Purpose, CV/Resume, Transcripts (unofficial accepted for application; official required if admitted), 3 letters of recommendation, application fee or waiver.
- **Optional or program-specific:** Personal History Statement (Stanford), Research Proposal (Europe), Writing Sample (some), Diversity Statement (some US programs require, others optional).
- **Recommender invitation:** You name recommenders in the application; the portal emails them a unique upload link. Most portals send the invitation immediately; some wait until you submit.
- **Submission confirmation:** Most portals send a confirmation email + show "Submitted" status on the dashboard.
- **Status check post-submission:** Bookmark the portal URL; check weekly for "complete" status (all documents received, all letters in).

## Per-program details

### 1. Stanford CS PhD

- **Portal URL:** https://gradadmissions.stanford.edu/ (verify; Stanford uses central Graduate Admissions for most departments)
- **Login:** Stanford Graduate Admissions account (separate from any other Stanford account)
- **Documents required:**
  - Statement of Purpose (1,000-1,500 words; portal may enforce a hard cap — verify)
  - **Personal History Statement** (1,500 words max; SEPARATE from SOP — Stanford is one of the few that requires both)
  - CV
  - Transcripts (unofficial PDF accepted)
  - 3 letters of recommendation
  - GRE: not required for CS as of 2024 cycle (verify for 2027-2028)
- **Recommender method:** Portal-direct invitation. Recommenders get a unique URL and upload PDF.
- **Application fee:** $125 typical. Fee waiver via Stanford's "Application Fee Waiver Request" — submission window often closes 2 weeks before the application deadline. Apply early.
- **Quirks:**
  - Stanford CS asks "intended research area" with a dropdown — pick the closest match (AI / NLP).
  - Personal History Statement is NOT the SOP — it's about your background, identity, and how it shaped your interest in research. Don't recycle the SOP.
  - GRE if submitted should be sent via ETS code (the program code is in the application; verify before sending).
- **Deadline:** Dec 1 typical for Fall entry [verify].
- **Common failure modes:** Submitting SOP in PHS slot; submitting before fee waiver approved (you pay twice).

### 2. CMU LTI PhD (and SCS PhD)

- **Portal URL:** https://www.lti.cs.cmu.edu/applying-lti-phd (verify); central CMU Graduate application at https://apply.cs.cmu.edu/
- **Login:** CMU Graduate Application account
- **Documents required:**
  - Statement of Purpose (~1,000-1,500 words; verify)
  - CV
  - Transcripts (unofficial PDF)
  - 3 letters of recommendation
  - **Writing sample** (LTI specifically — a research paper or technical report you wrote, ~10 pages; can be co-authored if you list contribution)
  - GRE: optional / not required (verify for 2027-2028)
- **Recommender method:** Portal-direct invitation via Interfolio integration (CMU accepts Interfolio dossiers).
- **Application fee:** $80 typical. Need-based waiver available.
- **Quirks:**
  - CMU LTI is one application form; you can also indicate interest in joint programs (e.g., LTI + ML, LTI + HCI).
  - Writing sample requirement is unusual; have one ready by Sep 2027.
  - The form asks you to rank up to 3 faculty advisors — list Šavelka primary, then Welleck and Fried.
  - If applying to multiple CMU CS departments (LTI + MLD + RI), only one fee — submit them together.
- **Deadline:** Dec 7 typical for LTI [verify; can vary year to year].

### 7. ETH Zürich CS PhD

- **Portal URL:** https://ethz.ch/en/doctorate/admission [verify]
- **Important:** ETH PhDs are often project-specific (advertised position). The general application process applies if no specific position is advertised; otherwise apply to the specific position via the supervisor's lab page.
- **Documents required:**
  - Motivation letter (Europe equivalent of SOP; ~2 pages typical)
  - CV
  - Transcripts (officially translated to English if not originally in English)
  - **Research proposal** (2-5 pages; Europe-standard requirement — write a focused version of CiteCheck design)
  - 2 letters of recommendation (Europe typically requires 2, not 3)
  - **Bachelor's + Master's degree certificates** (officially translated)
- **Recommender method:** Direct email or Interfolio.
- **Application fee:** ~$150 CHF (verify exchange rate).
- **Quirks:**
  - ETH application is sent to BOTH the central Doctoral Office AND the prospective supervisor (Mrinmaya Sachan or Ryan Cotterell). The supervisor's acceptance is the gating signal; central is a formality after that.
  - Email the prospective supervisor first (cold-email per `outreach/email_variants/`) before applying.
  - Funding is typically a 4-year position, not a stipend.
- **Deadline:** Rolling for project-specific positions; Dec 15 for the general doctoral application [verify].

## Common failure modes (across portals)

- **Browser issues:** Many portals only support Chrome / Firefox. Edge and Safari sometimes silently fail. Use Chrome by default. Test the upload flow on Day 1 of account creation, not on Day 1 of deadline.
- **PDF upload failures:** Many portals enforce 2-5 MB max per file. Compress with Ghostscript before uploading: `gs -sDEVICE=pdfwrite -dCompatibilityLevel=1.4 -dPDFSETTINGS=/ebook -dNOPAUSE -dQUIET -dBATCH -sOutputFile=output.pdf input.pdf`
- **Recommender invitations stuck in spam:** Tell your recommenders to whitelist `*.edu` and the specific portal's domain (e.g., `*.applyweb.com`, `*.embark.com`). Send a follow-up Slack/text within 24 hours of submitting the application to confirm.
- **Transcript order delays:** Most US institutions take 5-10 business days to process transcript requests. Order in early October at the latest.
- **Session timeouts:** Save work locally first. Most portals time out after 30 minutes of inactivity and dump unsaved work.
- **Submission button disabled:** Usually means one required field is incomplete. Scroll to the top — most portals highlight the missing field at the top of the form. If not visible, click through each section to find the red asterisk.
- **Status doesn't update after submission:** Some portals show "Submitted" but not "Complete" until all letters are in. The "Complete" status appears 1-7 days after the last letter is uploaded; don't panic.

## A `blockers.md` file to keep alongside this guide

Create `applications/submission/blockers.md` during Phase 4 to log portal-specific issues as they happen. Template:

```markdown
| Date | Program | Issue | Fix attempted | Resolved? |
|------|---------|-------|---------------|-----------|
| 2027-11-XX | Stanford CS | PDF upload rejected ">5MB" | Compressed via Ghostscript to 1.8 MB | Yes |
```

A live log of blockers becomes the single most valuable document during the application crunch; share it with anyone helping you apply.

## Templated email to program admissions (when something goes wrong)

```
Subject: [Program] PhD application — technical issue ahead of deadline

Dear [Program] Admissions,

I am preparing my application for the Fall 2028 PhD CS cycle (applicant: John Paul L. Gabule, applicant ID [if available]).

I am encountering [SPECIFIC ISSUE — e.g., "the SOP upload returns a 500 error", "the recommender invitation email is not arriving"] and have already tried [WHAT YOU TRIED]. The deadline is [DATE].

Could you advise on how to proceed? I want to ensure my application is submitted correctly and on time.

Thank you,
John Paul L. Gabule
johnpaullimgabule@gmail.com
```

Send this 1-2 weeks before the deadline, not on the day-of. Most admissions offices are responsive within 24-48 hours.
