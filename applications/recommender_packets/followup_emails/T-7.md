# Recommender Follow-up Email — T-7 (1 week before deadline)

**When to send:** Seven days before each deadline, ONLY to recommenders whose letter has not yet been submitted to that specific program.

**Purpose:** Gentle reminder. If they have submitted, do not send. If they have not submitted, this is a soft nudge with the portal link re-supplied.

**How to check before sending:** Most portals show letter-receipt status under "documents received" / "recommendations." Check each portal Sunday evening; send T-7 emails Monday morning for letters still missing.

---

**To:** [Recommender's email]
**Subject:** PhD letter — quick reminder for [Program] deadline [Date]

---

Dear Professor [Last Name],

Quick note: the [Program] deadline is [Date in recipient timezone] — one week from today. The portal still shows your letter as outstanding for this program.

If you have already uploaded and the portal is just lagging on the status update, please ignore this. (Some portals take 24-48 hours to display a received letter.)

If you have not yet uploaded, the portal link is:
[Portal upload link]

If the link is not working, or if you don't see the original invitation email, please tell me right away and I will re-send the invitation from the portal.

Thank you for everything you've done so far on the other programs — I have noted the letters that have come in.

Best,
John Paul L. Gabule
johnpaullimgabule@gmail.com

---

## Customization checklist

- [ ] Verify the letter is actually outstanding (don't send if portal shows received)
- [ ] Re-supply the unique portal upload link (find in your applicant dashboard)
- [ ] Send Monday morning recipient-time
- [ ] If still no response by T-2, escalate (see T-2.md)

## Tone notes

- Short. Three short paragraphs is the max.
- Hold-them-harmless framing ("if it's just portal lag, ignore this") makes them feel less rushed.
- Reference letters that did come in — shows you're keeping track and appreciative.
- DO NOT send to recommenders whose letter is already received. They will resent the noise.
