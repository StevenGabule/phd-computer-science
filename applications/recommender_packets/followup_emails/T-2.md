# Recommender Follow-up Email — T-2 (2 days before deadline)

**When to send:** Two days before deadline, ONLY to recommenders whose letter is still outstanding after the T-7 follow-up.

**Purpose:** Firm but still warm. This is the last polite reminder before escalation.

---

**To:** [Recommender's email]
**Subject:** URGENT: PhD letter for [Program] — [Date] deadline (48h)

---

Dear Professor [Last Name],

I am writing to confirm whether you'll be able to submit the letter for [Program] before the [Date] deadline (48 hours from now). The portal still shows it as outstanding.

I understand letter-writing on top of your own workload is a lot. If you cannot make this deadline, please tell me now — most programs offer 1-2 week extensions when the recommender contacts admissions directly to request it. I would much rather you ask for an extension than skip the letter.

Portal link (in case the original invitation got lost):
[Portal upload link]

Program admissions contact for an extension request:
[admissions email]

Subject line for the extension request (templated):
"Letter of recommendation extension request — John Paul L. Gabule, [Program ID]"

Body for the extension request (templated):
"I am writing a letter of recommendation for John Paul L. Gabule's PhD application. Due to [reason], I will need an additional [N] days to complete the letter. Please confirm whether this is acceptable for the [Program] PhD admissions cycle. — [Your Name]"

Please tell me which option you'd like: (a) submit by [Date], (b) request an extension, or (c) need help.

I'm available by phone at [number] if a 5-minute call would unstick anything.

Thank you,
John Paul L. Gabule
johnpaullimgabule@gmail.com

---

## Customization checklist

- [ ] Use only if letter is genuinely outstanding at T-2 (verify the portal again)
- [ ] Look up the program's admissions email and include it in the templated extension request
- [ ] Include your phone number — at T-2, async email may not be enough
- [ ] Send Tuesday/Wednesday recipient-time (avoid Friday evening); if deadline is Mon-Wed, send Sun-Mon
- [ ] If no response within 12 hours of T-2 email, move to escalation (T-escalation.md)

## Tone notes

- The subject line says "URGENT" because at T-2 it actually is. Don't soften this.
- Offer the extension option — most programs grant 1-2 week extensions automatically when the recommender asks. The recommender often doesn't know this.
- Include a templated extension-request body — reduces their cognitive load to almost zero.
- Offer the phone call. Recommenders sometimes ghost over email because they're embarrassed; a call breaks the loop.
- Do NOT guilt-trip. This is one of the cases where empathy outperforms pressure.
