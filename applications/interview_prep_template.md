# PhD Interview Prep Template

**Use period:** January – March 2028 (after applications submitted in Phase 4)
**Note:** Top US programs typically invite for interviews 2–8 weeks after submission. Europe varies widely (some interview immediately, some wait until decisions).

---

## Per-program interview prep packet

For each interview invitation, create a copy of this template at `applications/interviews/{program-name}.md`.

```markdown
# Interview — {Program Name}

**Date confirmed:** YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM (timezone)
**Format:** {Zoom / In-person / On-site visit / Multi-day open house}
**Interviewer(s):** [Names, titles, lab affiliation]
**Length:** [30-60 min typical; on-site days are 4-8 separate slots]

## Pre-interview research (do 48 hours before)
- [ ] Re-read the interviewer's 3 most recent papers (titles + 2-sentence summary each)
- [ ] Re-read the interviewer's lab page; identify 2-3 current PhD students whose work is closest to mine
- [ ] Re-read my own SOP for this program (last reviewed: YYYY-MM-DD)
- [ ] Re-read my problem_statement.md (locked at M2; updated YYYY-MM-DD)
- [ ] Look up 1 recent collaboration of the interviewer (last 2 years)
- [ ] Prepare 3 questions specific to their lab/program (NOT generic)

## My 60-second pitch (memorize, do not script-read)
[Open with the Mata v. Avianca hook — 1 sentence
Then: "I've been building CiteCheck, an open benchmark and method..." — 2 sentences
Then: "I'd want to extend this during a PhD to..." — 1 sentence
Total: 4 sentences, 60 seconds spoken at normal pace]

## Specific connection to this lab
[1-2 sentences citing a specific paper or project, e.g., "Your 2027 paper on X applied to Y resonates with CiteCheck's support-axis NLI work, and I'd be curious how you handled the Z trade-off."]

## My 3 questions for them (write 5, pick 3 on the day based on flow)
1. [Specific to their current research direction or lab challenge]
2. [Specific to PhD student experience at this program — funding, advising style, qual exams]
3. [Specific to a recent paper of theirs — methodological choice or future extension]
4. [Reserve]
5. [Reserve]

## Likely questions to be ready for
- "Tell me about your background and what brings you here."
- "Walk me through your research project."
- "Why this program / why this lab?"
- "What would you want to work on if I admitted you?"
- "Describe a technical challenge you've overcome."
- "Why a PhD instead of industry?"
- "What's your 5-year vision?"
- "What questions do you have for me?"

## Post-interview (within 24 hours)
- [ ] Send thank-you email (1 paragraph, specific reference to one thing discussed)
- [ ] Log interview in `applications/interviews/log.md`: date, interviewer, vibe, follow-up needed
- [ ] Update `journal/decisions.md` if interview changed my ranking of the program
```

## General preparation (do before any interviews start, Jan 2028)

- [ ] **Speaking practice:** record myself answering the 8 likely questions; review for verbal tics ("um", "like"), pacing, eye contact (for video).
- [ ] **Whiteboard practice:** be ready to sketch CiteCheck's three-stage architecture on a virtual whiteboard or paper (without notes). Practice 3 versions: 30-second, 2-minute, 5-minute.
- [ ] **Code walkthrough:** be ready to navigate to one specific file in the CiteCheck repo and explain a design decision. Pick the CitationResolver tool as the most-likely-asked-about component.
- [ ] **Limitations:** prepare honest 1-sentence answers for the 3 known weaknesses (NLI noise on legal language, state-court coverage, latency).
- [ ] **Failure stories:** have 2 specific examples of research approaches that didn't work and what you learned. Avoid generic "I learned to be persistent."
- [ ] **Off-the-record questions:** prepare what you'd ask a current PhD student over coffee (advising style, funding stability, work-life balance, post-PhD outcomes).

## Red flags to watch for during interviews (other side of the table)

- Advisor says they're "fully committed" or "not taking students this cycle" — interview is a courtesy, not a real offer.
- No current PhD students mention the advisor in published recent papers — possible they've stopped advising.
- Group meetings happen <weekly — light advising relationship; may suit some, not others.
- Funding only guaranteed for first year — verify multi-year support.
- Advisor's last student took >7 years to graduate — check median time-to-degree.

## Logistics

- **Have multiple devices ready:** primary laptop + phone backup for Zoom failures.
- **Test microphone, camera, internet 30 min before each call.**
- **Quiet, well-lit space; neutral background.**
- **Have a glass of water and a pen + paper for notes within reach.**
- **Notes app open on second screen with the prep packet** (do not read from it; use as glance reference).

## Calendar template for interview season

- **Jan 5–15:** First wave of invites (top-tier US programs interviewing fastest).
- **Jan 15–Feb 15:** Bulk of interviews; some on-site days (multi-day, 1-2 day commitment each).
- **Feb 15–Mar 15:** Second wave (waitlist conversion interviews, Europe programs).
- **Mar 15–Apr 15:** Decision wave; April 15 is the typical US PhD admit-by-date.

Block calendar in 90-minute windows for each potential interview slot. Many programs send <72 hr notice; have availability ready.
