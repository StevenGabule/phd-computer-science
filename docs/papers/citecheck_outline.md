## 1. Introduction (~1 page)

### 1.1 Opening anecdote — Mata v. Avianca

Draft prose:

> In June 2023, Judge P. Kevin Castel of the Southern District of New York sanctioned two attorneys after they filed a brief containing six entirely fabricated case citations — "Varghese v. China Southern Airlines," "Shaboon v. EgyptAir," and four others — generated by ChatGPT and presented as binding precedent (*Mata v. Avianca, Inc.*, 22-cv-1461, S.D.N.Y. June 22, 2023). The opinions did not exist. Neither did the reporter volumes that purportedly contained them.

- Use this to motivate why "hallucinated case citation" is not an abstract NLP failure mode but a sanctionable professional-responsibility failure that has measurably injured real litigants and lawyers.

### 1.2 Scale of the problem

Draft prose:

> The Mata sanction was not an isolated incident. The Damien Charlotin tracker has logged on the order of fifty US court orders since 2023 that flag AI-generated fabricated citations, and the most rigorous empirical audit to date, Magesh et al. (2025), found that even commercial legal-research products marketed as "hallucination-free" — Lexis+ AI, Thomson Reuters Westlaw AI-Assisted Research, and Practical Law's Ask — produce incorrect or unsupported information on 17% to 33% of legal queries.

- Cite: Magesh et al. (2025), *J. Empirical Legal Studies* 22(2), 216-242, https://doi.org/10.1111/jels.12413.
- Cite: Charlotin AI-hallucination tracker (URL TBD; cite as a dataset/web resource).

### 1.3 Why open and reproducible matters

Draft prose:

> The populations most exposed to fabrication harm — junior associates billed out at first-year rates, public defenders, pro-bono and *pro se* litigants — are also the populations least able to afford the proprietary tools Magesh et al. audited, and least able to retain a senior partner to vet every cited authority. Cheong et al. (2025) document, through interviews with public defenders, that AI adoption in legal services is gated less by appetite than by cost, auditability, and the asymmetric reputational risk of a single bad citation.

- Cite: Cheong et al. (2025), https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2510.22933.

### 1.4 What is missing in existing work

- Magesh et al. (2025) is closed: no benchmark, no baselines, no method — only an empirical audit.
- EL-RAG (Wankhade 2026; https://doi.org/10.21203/rs.3.rs-8947587/v1) is an open legal RAG with an Evidence Alignment Layer but evaluates on COLIEE / NyayaRAG / CaseLawBench, none of which are US case-law focused; the paper is closed-access and there is no public code.
- LegalBench (Guha et al. 2022; arxiv:2209.06120) and LegalBench-RAG (Pipitone & Houir Alami 2024; arxiv:2408.10343) are open and US-focused but do not isolate citation faithfulness as a scored axis.
- HalluGraph (Noël et al. 2025; arxiv:2512.01659) and CiteGuard (Choi et al. 2025; arxiv:2510.17853) verify citations post-hoc against a knowledge graph or a retrieval set, but neither queries a live legal-opinion registry inside the generation loop.

### 1.5 Contributions

State three:

1. **Benchmark.** CiteCheck v0.1, ~500 US-grounded legal questions paired with gold citations and per-axis labels for existence, support, and jurisdictional validity. Released CC-BY with prompts, splits, and adversarial seed provenance from the Charlotin tracker.
2. **Method.** A three-stage agentic-RAG pipeline whose two novel components are (a) a cross-encoder reranker trained with a Bluebook-structure-aware groundedness loss `L = L_relevance + λ * L_groundedness`, parsed via `eyecite`; and (b) an agent verify loop that emits citations through a constrained-decoding Bluebook grammar and resolves them at decode time against CourtListener with a local CAP mirror as fallback.
3. **Empirical study.** Comparison against five open baselines (Vanilla 8B, Naive RAG, Self-RAG, CRAG, EL-RAG reimplementation), one closed reference (GPT-4o-mini with web search), and one post-hoc detector (HalluGraph), plus four ablations isolating the contribution of each pipeline stage.

### 1.6 Paper roadmap (1-2 sentences)

- "Section 2 surveys the four bodies of work this paper draws on; Section 3 specifies the benchmark; Section 4 specifies the method; Sections 5-6 report experimental setup and results; Sections 7-8 discuss limitations, ethics, and future work."

Draft prose:

### 2.3 Citation faithfulness in general LLMs

Draft prose:

> Outside law, ALCE (Gao et al. 2023; https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/2023.emnlp-main.398) established the modern formulation of citation-aware generation by treating citation correctness as a scored output property; subsequent work — Ji et al. (2024) on chain-of-thought citation, Wu et al. (2025) on SourceCheckup for medical references (https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-58551-6), and Choi et al. ### 2.4 Agentic RAG architectures

Draft prose:

> Self-RAG (Asai et al. 2023; arxiv:2310.11511) introduced self-reflective control tokens for retrieve/no-retrieve and support-level decisions; CRAG (Yan et al. 2024) added a retrieval evaluator that triggers corrective re-retrieval; Adaptive-RAG (Jeong et al. 2024; https://doi.org/10.18653/v1/2024.naacl-long.389) adapts retrieval depth to estimated question complexity. More recent planner-centric work — Beyond ReAct (Wei et al. 2025; arxiv:2511.10037), ReCAP (Zhang et al. 2025; arxiv:2510.23822), and Lateral Tree-of-Thoughts (Madahar 2025; arxiv:2510.01500) — improves the planning loop on general-domain agent benchmarks. None of these architectures has been instantiated with a legal-specific verification tool such as a live opinion-registry lookup.

### 2.5 Hallucination detection in legal RAG

Draft prose:

> The two empirical works most directly adjacent to CiteCheck are Magesh et al. (2025; https://doi.org/10.1111/jels.12413), which audits closed commercial tools, and HalluGraph (Noël et al. 2025; arxiv:2512.01659), which proposes auditable hallucination detection for legal RAG via knowledge-graph alignment. Magesh is closed: no released benchmark or method. HalluGraph is open but operates as a post-hoc detector on top of an arbitrary generator, not as a generation-time verifier. CiteCheck differs from Magesh by being fully open and reproducible, and from HalluGraph by closing the loop at decode time.

- One sentence pointer to Munir, Abbasi & Wilson (2025; https://doi.org/10.1017/jli.2025.10052) as a comparative-products audit that complements Magesh.

## 3. The CiteCheck Benchmark

### 3.1 The three axes

For each axis, give a one-paragraph definition and a worked example (a question + a candidate citation labeled positive on one axis, negative on another).

- **Existence.** Does the cited authority (case name + reporter + court + year) resolve to a real opinion in CourtListener (with CAP mirror fallback)? Binary. Machine-checkable.
- **Support.** Given the proposition the answer asserts the citation stands for, does the resolved opinion text actually entail or substantiate that proposition? Three-valued {entails, neutral, contradicts}; binarized for primary metric.
- **Jurisdictional validity.** Given the forum and legal question, is the cited court binding, persuasive, or off-point for the question? Three-valued {binding, persuasive, off-point}.

### 3.2 Dataset construction methodology

- **Seed:** Adversarial questions seeded from the Charlotin AI-hallucination tracker. For each logged sanction order, extract the underlying legal question and one fabricated citation; convert to a question + (negative) citation pair.
- **Corpus side:** Caselaw Access Project bulk download (~6.7M opinions) and CourtListener API for verification and metadata. See `citecheck/src/citecheck/data/cap_loader.py` and `citecheck/src/citecheck/data/cl_client.py`.
- **LLM pre-label:** GPT-4o-mini drafts axis labels for ~1,500 candidate question/citation pairs (Gray et al. 2024 methodology for LLM-pre-labeling).
- **Human verify:** Subset to ~500 pairs for human-verified gold; double-annotate 100 for inter-annotator agreement. See `citecheck/src/citecheck/data/eval_set.py`.

### 3.3 Coverage

- ~500 questions for v0.1 (cap chosen against solo-annotation capacity; see Section 7.4).
- Court tiers: federal appellate (Circuit Courts) + US Supreme Court. State appellate deferred to v0.2 per the M2 lock decision.
- Topical coverage: civil procedure, contracts (overlap with CUAD), constitutional law, criminal procedure. Specific distribution table `<TBD>`.

## 4. Method: The CiteCheck Pipeline

### 4.1 Architecture overview

Figure 1 (block diagram): question → Stage 1 hybrid retrieve → Stage 2 faithfulness-tuned rerank → Stage 3 agent verify loop → annotated answer.

Notes for Phase 3:
- Implementation lives in `citecheck/src/citecheck/`, organized as `retrieval/`, `reranker/`, `agent/`, `baselines/`, `data/`, `eval/`.
- Refer reader to the design specification `project/citecheck_design.md` for full hyperparameter and training details.

### 4.2 Stage 1: Hybrid retrieval

- **Sparse.** BM25 via pyserini over the local CAP bulk download. Source: `citecheck/src/citecheck/retrieval/bm25.py`.
- **Dense.** Dual encoder `BAAI/bge-base-en-v1.5`, A/B compared against `nlpaueb/legal-bert-base-uncased`. Source: `citecheck/src/citecheck/retrieval/dense.py`.
- **Fusion.** Reciprocal Rank Fusion over top-50 from each branch, returning top-20. Source: `citecheck/src/citecheck/retrieval/fusion.py`.
- Cite: Lewis et al. (2020) for the RAG framing; Robertson & Zaragoza (2009) for BM25 as a one-liner; Xiao et al. (2024) for the BGE encoder family (citation TBD on the specific bge-base release).

### 4.3 Stage 2: Faithfulness-tuned reranker

Draft sketch:

> The reranker is a cross-encoder fine-tuned with a two-term loss: `L = L_relevance + λ * L_groundedness`. `L_relevance` is the standard pointwise relevance objective. `L_groundedness` is a binary cross-entropy on whether the candidate passage *contains a Bluebook-parseable citation whose resolved opinion text supports the asserted claim*, parsed via the `eyecite` Bluebook-citation parser (Free Law Project). We sweep `λ ∈ {0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 1.0}` on the development split.

- Mining of (query, doc, label) triples: 10k-30k triples from CiteCheck dev split + Stage-1 BM25 candidates as hard/easy negatives. Source: `citecheck/src/citecheck/reranker/dataset.py`, `loss.py`, `model.py`, `train.py`.
- Cite as methodological prior: Guan et al. (2025) faithfulness-aware multi-objective context ranking (10.20944/preprints202512.1983.v1); Khan et al. (2026) Reason and Verify (arxiv:2603.10143); Li et al. (2023) PARADE for passage representation aggregation (https://doi.org/10.1145/3600088).
- Novelty: the use of the *Bluebook citation structure* itself — parsed reporter/court/year — as a groundedness signal is, to our reading of the literature, novel.

### 4.4 Stage 3: Agent verify loop

Sub-components, with code references:

- **Constrained decoding.** A Bluebook grammar specified for the `outlines` library covers full case cites, short cites, `id.`, `supra`, and parallel cites; v0.1 grammar covers roughly 80% of common US-case-cite forms encountered in the CAP sample (residual 20% logged and reported). Source: `citecheck/src/citecheck/agent/grammar.py`.
- **`CitationResolver` tool.** Queries CourtListener API; falls back to local CAP mirror on rate-limit. Returns `(exists, opinion_text, court, year)`. Source: `citecheck/src/citecheck/agent/citation_resolver.py`. Cache layer described in design spec.
- **Verify loop.** Max-k=3 iterations of (retrieve → generate → resolve → entail-check → re-retrieve-on-failure). Citations are tagged `verified`, `unresolvable`, or `non_supporting` in the final answer. Source: `citecheck/src/citecheck/agent/loop.py`.
- Cite for constrained decoding: Willard & Louf (2023) on `outlines` (cite as the arXiv preprint); Schall & de Melo (2025) on the trade-offs of constrained decoding (https://doi.org/10.26615/978-954-452-098-4-124) — flag the latency/quality trade-off they document.
- Cite for agent verify loop precedent: Asai et al. (2023) Self-RAG; Yan et al. (2024) CRAG; Anand et al. (2026) Self-CRAG (https://doi.org/10.4018/979-8-3373-2721-1.ch008).

### 4.5 Novelty discussion

Two paragraphs:

- **Live-registry verification vs. post-hoc detection.** Magesh (2025) and HalluGraph (Noël et al. 2025) operate on completed model outputs. CiteCheck instead queries CourtListener inside the generation loop, so unresolvable citations trigger a retraction-or-retry before the user sees them. This converts citation faithfulness from a downstream audit into a generation-time hard constraint.
- **Structure-aware reranking.** Existing faithfulness rerankers (Guan 2025, Khan 2026) score relevance and faithfulness over free text. CiteCheck's reranker additionally exploits the typed-field structure exposed by `eyecite` — reporter, court, year, pinpoint — so the loss can attend to whether the *correct kind* of authority appears in the candidate passage, not merely a textually similar one.

## 7. Discussion

### 7.1 What did and did not beat the baselines

- Sketch the expected narrative: agentic verify loop should dominate on Fabrication Rate and Citation Resolution Rate; ablations should show the reranker contributing most to Citation Support F1 and the CitationResolver contributing most to Fabrication Rate.
- Honest contingency to draft: if CRAG or Self-RAG already drives Fabrication Rate to near zero on this benchmark, reframe contribution around the Bluebook-aware reranker and per-axis decomposition rather than headline reduction.

### 7.2 Open vs. closed

- Where CiteCheck matches or exceeds GPT-4o-mini-with-web-search, emphasize: this is an open-weight 7-8B model on a single 24 GB GPU, with no proprietary API.
- Where GPT-4o-mini wins, be honest about which axis and discuss whether the gap closes with the secondary backbone or the 13B stretch.

### 7.3 Implications for legal-tech deployment

- Mata-type harm: a verify loop that returns "unresolvable citation" rather than a confident fabrication is the minimum bar a deployed system must clear.
- Audit story: per-citation verification annotations (verified / unresolvable / non-supporting) provide the audit trail that Magesh's audit found absent in closed commercial tools.

### 7.4 Limitations

Per the Phase 2 plan, label this section "Limitations" and include at minimum:

- **State coverage.** v0.1 restricts to federal appellate + Supreme Court; state appellate is v0.2 work given uneven CourtListener coverage.
- **NLI proxy.** Legal "stands for" is stronger than textual entailment; we report human-audited Support F1 alongside NLI.
- **Grammar coverage.** v0.1 Bluebook grammar covers ~80% of common case-cite forms; unsupported forms (string cites, signal-heavy parentheticals, foreign parallel cites) logged in Appendix A.
- **Latency overhead.** Verify loop adds an estimated 10-30% p95 (`<TBD>` exact); deployment tolerance depends on chat vs. brief-prep use case.
- **Language and jurisdiction scope.** US English only; portability to EU/UK/Indian corpora (cf. EL-RAG's COLIEE, NyayaRAG) not demonstrated.
- **Annotator pool.** Small (`<TBD>` total); uneven practice-area coverage (constitutional > administrative, civ-pro > trusts-and-estates).

### 7.5 Ethical considerations

- **Dual-use.** A fabrication benchmark could be repurposed for adversarial fine-tuning. Mitigation: license terms, README advisory, DOI-citable methodology.
- **Seed provenance.** Charlotin tracker entries derive from public court orders; we cite docket numbers without re-publishing attorney names beyond the public record.
- **Not legal advice.** The benchmark and method do not constitute legal advice; deployment guidance in Appendix D.
