# Sustainable Practices

**Purpose:** Stay healthy enough to complete the 18-month plan. The most common failure mode in PhD-prep is not lack of intelligence or work ethic — it's burnout in month 12-14.

**Principle:** the plan only works if you make it to month 18 in a state where you can actually take interviews. Heroic month-8 sprints that leave you exhausted by month 14 are net-negative.

---

## The non-negotiables

If you read nothing else here, do these:

1. **Sleep 7-8 hours/night, every night, for the entire 18 months.** Tired you produces worse work and worse decisions. The "all-nighter to meet a deadline" is almost always a worse outcome than turning in the work 12 hours late.
2. **One full day off per week.** No reading, no writing, no code, no email. Genuinely off.
3. **One full week off per quarter.** Travel, sleep, see family, do anything else. The 18-month plan has slack built in for 4 such weeks.
4. **One social commitment per week.** Dinner, a hobby group, anything non-research. Solitary research without social input is a slow path to depression.
5. **Physical movement 30+ min, 4+ days/week.** The data on this is overwhelming. Even walking counts.

If you violate one of these for more than 2 weeks consecutively, something is wrong with your week-to-week pacing. Revisit and correct.

## Phase-specific intensity calibration

Not every phase is equally intense.

| Phase | Months | Intensity | Hours/week target | Sustainable? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 - Foundation | May-Aug 2026 | Medium | 35-40 | Yes, with weekends off |
| 2 - Build | Sep 2026 - Apr 2027 | High in Mar-Apr | 35-45 (peaks 50) | Yes; protect 1 day/wk + 1 wk/quarter |
| 3 - Publish & Apply | May-Sep 2027 | High in Aug | 40-50 | Borderline; protect sleep ruthlessly |
| 4 - Application Push | Oct-Dec 2027 | Very high in Nov-Dec | 50-60 (peaks 70) | NOT sustainable; needs full recovery week after |
| 5 - Interview & Decide | Jan-Apr 2028 | Variable | 25-40 (interview weeks higher) | Sustainable with calendar discipline |

**Plan for the peaks.** The Nov-Dec 2027 final push is the only window where 60+ hour weeks are reasonable. Everything else should be 35-45.

**Plan for the recovery.** Jan 2028 (after Dec deadlines) needs a hard 5-7 day off. Don't open a portal, don't refresh a status page.

## Detecting burnout early

Burnout is not "I'm tired today." It's a structural state that requires structural correction.

**Early signs (1-2 weeks of these, take action):**
- Dread on Sunday evening about Monday's work
- Procrastinating on things that should take 10 minutes
- Reduced quality of work output (more errors, less synthesis)
- Trouble sleeping despite feeling exhausted
- Less interest in topics that previously excited you
- Avoiding the reading group / paper-swap / social check-ins

**Late signs (immediate full break):**
- Cynicism about the entire PhD path
- Inability to start any work for multiple days
- Physical symptoms: persistent fatigue, headaches, gastrointestinal issues
- Loss of appetite or sleep regulation
- Isolating from friends and family

**If you see late signs:** take 7-14 days completely off. Do not "work on something easier" — that's not recovery. Genuinely off. If symptoms persist beyond 2 weeks, talk to a therapist or doctor.

## Practical week-to-week protocols

### Monday morning ritual

- Re-read last week's `journal/weekly.md` entry
- Open the current phase plan; identify this week's primary task
- Open `roadmap/THE_ROADMAP.md`; check the current week's expectations
- Write one sentence in `journal/daily.md` about what success looks like this week

### Daily focus block

- 90-minute focus block in the morning (your peak cognitive window)
- Phone in another room, laptop in airplane mode for non-research tabs
- One task during the block; if you finish early, take a real break before starting another
- Pomodoro (25/5) if 90-minute blocks feel too long

### Friday afternoon close-out

- 15-minute review: what did I complete this week?
- Update `journal/weekly.md` weekly entry
- Pre-plan Monday's first task (eliminates Monday-morning friction)
- Then close the laptop until Sunday evening at earliest

### Sunday evening planning

- 30-minute weekly planning session
- Look at next week's phase-plan tasks
- Identify the "one hard thing" — the highest-priority task that, if it slips, blocks others
- Block calendar time for the hard thing (mornings, when energy is highest)

## Coffee, alcohol, screens

### Coffee
- 1-2 cups/day, before noon
- Avoid the 3pm "I need an extra cup" — that's a sleep-debt signal, not a caffeine deficit
- If you're consistently >3 cups, you're treating a symptom of poor sleep

### Alcohol
- Reduces sleep quality even at low doses
- 1-2 drinks/week max during high-intensity phases
- Zero during Nov-Dec 2027 (application push) — sleep is too valuable

### Screens
- No screens 60 minutes before bed (the blue-light research is mixed but the dopamine effects are clear)
- Phone notifications off during focus blocks
- Twitter / Bluesky / Reddit: time-boxed (15 minutes/day max) — see `community/networking_strategy.md` for the constructive use

### Other substances
- Modafinil / Adderall / non-prescribed stimulants: don't. The short-term cognitive boost is real; the long-term effects on a multi-year plan are net-negative.
- If you have a legitimate prescription, talk to your prescriber about the 18-month timeline. They may want to adjust.

## Movement and exercise

Don't optimize. The best exercise is the one you'll do consistently for 18 months.

**Sustainable patterns (pick one):**
- 30-min walk daily (lowest barrier, surprisingly effective)
- 2-3x/week strength training (45 min sessions)
- 2-3x/week cardio (running, cycling, swimming, 30-45 min)
- A team sport (tennis, basketball, recreational league) 1-2x/week
- Yoga or pilates 2-3x/week

**What to avoid:**
- Hour-long workouts you skip half the time (90-min consistent < 30-min daily)
- "I'll exercise after the deadline" — the deadlines never end
- Comparison to peak-fitness people (this is maintenance, not transformation)

## Diet (the boring stuff that matters)

You don't need a diet plan. You need to avoid the failure modes:

**Failure modes to avoid:**
- Skipping breakfast in favor of "more coffee"
- Lunch from a delivery app 5 days/week
- Dinner skipped because "I'll grab something later"
- Hydration: <1L of water/day in addition to coffee

**Default protocol:**
- 3 meals/day, at roughly the same times
- One meal at home each weekday (eat lunch out, dinner at home, or vice versa)
- Vegetables at every meal (even small portions)
- Water bottle on your desk; refill 3+ times/day

The Phase 4 push (Nov-Dec 2027) is when this slips most. Pre-cook or meal-prep on Sundays for that window.

## Sleep — the single highest-leverage variable

If you can only fix one thing, fix sleep.

**Targets:**
- 7.5-9 hours/night (most people; calibrate to your individual need)
- Consistent bedtime within ±60 minutes (more than wake time)
- Dark, cool, quiet room

**Common sleep-debt patterns and fixes:**

| Pattern | Fix |
|---|---|
| "Just one more paragraph" → bedtime drifts to 1am | Hard cutoff at 11pm; lights out 11:30pm |
| "Can't fall asleep, mind racing" | Brain dump on paper 30 min before bed; no problem-solving in bed |
| "Wake at 3am thinking about Phase 2" | Same brain-dump protocol; if persistent, journaling 5 min before bed |
| Weekend sleep-in 3+ hours later than weekday | The shift damages sleep quality more than people realize; cap weekend wake at +1 hr |
| Caffeine after 2pm | Cut to 0; substitute green tea (lower caffeine, less half-life) |

**When sleep doesn't improve despite the above:** talk to a doctor. Sleep apnea is dramatically underdiagnosed and devastatingly under-treated. Worth a sleep study if symptoms persist.

## Relationships and family

The 18-month plan affects your partner, family, close friends. Anticipate this.

**Communication protocols:**

- **Tell your partner the plan in detail.** Show them this document. Let them adjust expectations about the next 18 months.
- **Set a weekly "no work" day with people you live with.** Phones away, no laptop, present.
- **Negotiate the application push specifically.** Nov-Dec 2027 will be the hardest on relationships. Plan a real break together in January 2028.
- **Family: send updates monthly.** Brief — 1 paragraph — but consistent. Reduces the "you've become a ghost" complaint.

**Failure mode:** treating relationships as a "I'll get to them after the deadline." There is always a next deadline. People give up on you.

**Constructive use of relationships:**
- Partner / spouse: hear you talk through hard decisions
- Family: emotional support during low points
- Close friends: perspective when you're too deep in the plan to see clearly

## Mental health

The PhD-prep cycle is genuinely hard on mental health. Some statistics worth knowing:

- ~30% of PhD students experience clinical depression at some point during their program
- The pre-application year (your situation) has elevated risk because of high uncertainty + isolation
- Burnout symptoms are not character weakness; they are physiological responses to sustained chronic stress

**When to seek professional help:**
- 2+ weeks of persistent low mood or anxiety
- Sleep disrupted despite addressing the lifestyle factors
- Loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy
- Thoughts of self-harm (immediate; call a crisis line)

**Where to get help:**
- University counseling center if you're still affiliated
- Insurance-covered therapy via Psychology Today's directory
- Crisis lines: 988 (US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
- Online therapy: BetterHelp, Talkspace (mixed reviews but accessible)

**Stigma in CS / academia:** real but decreasing. Most senior researchers have been there. Don't suffer in silence because of academic culture.

## The "yes, but I have a deadline" objection

Most people read this kind of document and think: "yes, but I have a deadline; I can't take a day off."

**Response:** the deadline is fixed. Your output capacity is variable. Working 70 hours instead of 50 in week N produces ~10% more output and ~30% less in week N+1. Net: less total output, more exhaustion.

The math is hard to internalize but the data is clear. Knowledge work has sharply diminishing returns past ~50 hours/week.

## A concrete sustainable schedule (example)

Not prescriptive — adapt to your life. But as a baseline:

**Weekdays:**
- 7:00 wake, 30-min morning routine (no phone)
- 7:30 breakfast + light exercise (walk, stretching)
- 8:30 deep work block 1 (~90 min)
- 10:00 break (15 min, real break)
- 10:15 deep work block 2 (~90 min)
- 11:45 lunch (full break, off-screen)
- 13:00 deep work block 3 (~90 min)
- 14:30 break (30 min walk)
- 15:00 admin / email / meetings (1-2 hr)
- 16:30 lighter work (planning, reading) (90 min)
- 18:00 close laptop
- 18:00-22:30 dinner, social time, relationship time, hobby
- 22:30 wind-down (no screens after this)
- 23:00 lights out

Total focused work: ~7-8 hours/day × 5 days = 35-40 hours/week. Sustainable.

**Saturday:** off entirely. Treat it as Sunday.

**Sunday:**
- Real morning off
- 14:00 weekly review (30 min)
- 14:30 plan next week (30 min)
- 15:00-evening: off again

## The closing reminder

The 18-month plan exists to get you into a PhD program where you'll spend 4-6 more years doing this kind of work. If the plan breaks you before you arrive, the PhD won't happen.

Pace, not heroics. Health, not optimization. The boring sustainable approach beats the exciting unsustainable one every single time over 18 months.

If you have to choose between "do the work" and "stay healthy enough to keep doing the work," choose the latter. Always.
