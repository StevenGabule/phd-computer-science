## Problem (3-4 sentences)

Open-weight legal RAG systems routinely emit case citations that look syntactically correct in Bluebook form ("Smith v. Jones, 412 F.3d 567 (9th Cir. 2005)") but refer to cases that do not exist, do not stand for the proposition cited, or come from the wrong jurisdiction. In Mata v. Avianca (S.D.N.Y. 2023), two attorneys were sanctioned for filing a brief containing six such fabricated citations generated by ChatGPT; the Charlotin AI-hallucination tracker has since logged roughly fifty US court orders involving fabricated AI-generated authorities. Magesh et al. (2025) showed that even proprietary legal RAG tools (Lexis+ AI, Westlaw AI-Assisted Research) hallucinate 17-33% of the time, but they release no open benchmark, no open-weight baseline, and no method. Existing open frameworks (e.g., EL-RAG, Wankhade 2026) evaluate on non-US corpora (COLIEE, NyayaRAG, CaseLawBench) and do not resolve citations against a public US registry.

## Why this matters

The Mata sanction has been followed by judges adding standing orders requiring AI-use disclosure; legal-tech vendors face liability for false citations; and public defenders / pro-bono attorneys who would most benefit from open-weight legal RAG (per Cheong et al. 2025) cannot adopt these tools without auditability. Without an open, reproducible US-case-law-grounded benchmark and an open method to drive the fabrication rate down, the field cedes the problem to closed proprietary vendors whose error rates remain unverifiable. Failure mode: a junior associate, public defender, or pro-bono attorney drafts a brief citing six cases that look right but never existed.

## Why agentic RAG is the right approach

Static RAG retrieves passages but cannot verify whether the emitted citations within the generated text correspond to real opinions — the generator may invent a plausible Bluebook citation that resembles retrieved cases without matching any of them. A single LLM call produces a one-shot answer with no opportunity to retract on failure-to-resolve. Agentic RAG adds a CitationResolver tool that queries CourtListener at decode-time, plus a retraction-or-re-retrieval loop (max k iterations), turning citation faithfulness into a constraint enforced during generation rather than a property checked after the fact.

## Proposed approach

A three-stage pipeline over a Llama-3.1-8B / Qwen2.5-7B base with QLoRA. **Stage 1 — Retrieve:** hybrid BM25 + dense (BGE-legal) over a Caselaw Access Project / CourtListener-indexed corpus. **Stage 2 — Faithfulness-tuned rerank:** a cross-encoder fine-tuned with a multi-objective loss combining standard relevance and a structure-aware citation-grounding signal (Bluebook fields parsed via `eyecite`); positive pairs require the candidate passage to contain a citation whose resolved opinion text supports the claim. **Stage 3 — Agent verify:** at generation time the model emits citations through a constrained-decoding grammar; a CitationResolver agent tool queries the CourtListener API and a local CAP mirror; unresolvable or non-entailing citations trigger retraction or re-retrieval (max k iterations). Novel contributions sit at the rerank loss (Bluebook-structure-aware) and the agent tool-loop (live registry verification).

## Evaluation plan

**Datasets:** Caselaw Access Project bulk data (~6.7M public US opinions), CourtListener API for live verification, LegalBench-RAG (Pipitone 2024) as task source, CUAD for contract-citation subset. Need to construct: ~500-1000 question/gold-citation pairs (seed from Charlotin tracker briefs for adversarial cases; LLM pre-label + human verification).

**Baselines:** Vanilla Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (no retrieval), Llama-3.1-8B + naive BM25 RAG, Self-RAG (Asai et al. 2024), CRAG (Yan et al. 2024), EL-RAG reimplementation (if feasible), HalluGraph as post-hoc detector, GPT-4o-mini with web-search as closed-API ceiling reference.

**Metrics:** Citation Resolution Rate (% of emitted citations that resolve to a real CourtListener opinion — machine-checkable), Citation Support F1 (does the resolved opinion entail the asserted proposition; NLI-judged + 200-item human audit), Jurisdictional Validity (% from binding/persuasive jurisdiction), Fabrication Rate (1 − resolution rate; primary headline tied to Mata-type harm), Answer Utility (Likert 1-5, 100-item human audit) to detect over-retraction, latency and tokens-per-query.

**Ablations:** without reranker, without CitationResolver tool, without constrained decoding; per-jurisdiction breakdown; multiple base models if compute allows.

## Risks and contingencies

- **Annotation effort balloons** beyond solo capacity. *Mitigation:* seed from Charlotin tracker briefs (zero-cost adversarial examples); LLM pre-labels with spot-check methodology (Gray et al. 2024); release v0.1 with 500 items rather than 1000.
- **Scoop risk.** Butler & Butler 2026 (Legal RAG Bench) is the closest competitor; Magesh follow-up possible; CiteGuard 2025 (scientific) could extend to legal. *Mitigation:* differentiate on open-weight + open-data + reproducible-on-one-GPU + agent-tool-loop combination; emphasize live-registry verification as the methodological contribution.
- **CourtListener API rate limits** break the agent loop at scale. *Mitigation:* mirror the CAP bulk dump locally (~100 GB), cache resolver calls, batch.
- **Entailment NLI is noisy on legal language.** *Mitigation:* report both NLI score and human-audited subset; do not headline NLI-only numbers; calibrate against legal experts when possible.
- **No law-student annotator available.** *Mitigation:* fall back to Charlotin tracker + appellate-brief mining for gold citations; explore Upwork JD reviewers (~$2k–$3.2k cash budget per Phase 2 plan estimate).
